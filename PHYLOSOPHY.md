# Dopamine120 Philosophy

## Idea

Dopamine120 is a dopamine trainer, not a guilt tracker.

The app does not measure the user's dopamine state, and it does not try to slow anything down or speed anything up. Instead it uses dopamine the way the brain already uses it: as a learning signal. By pairing deliberate effort with a consciously chosen reward, the app teaches the brain that good feelings can come from things other than scrolling.

The app does not try to ban pleasure. It helps the user move from impulse to conscious action.

## Core Loop

1. **30 minutes of deprivation**
   A short mode without fast stimuli. The goal is to clear the noise before effort, not punish the user.

2. **Two minutes to think about the task**
   Before focus, the user looks at the task, names the next step, and lowers the resistance to starting.

3. **25 minutes of focus on an unpleasant task**
   This is work on something the user tends to avoid. The session can be interrupted at any time. Interrupting is not failure; every minute of deliberate effort is a repetition that trains the brain.

4. **Optional app blocking**
   During focus, the user can block selected apps, but this is not required. Blocking is environmental support, not the main mechanism.

5. **A conscious pleasant action**
   After focus, the app suggests doing something pleasant. The user can choose an option, type their own, or skip. This is the heart of the training: the reward comes right after effort, so the brain learns to associate dopamine with finishing hard things instead of with scrolling. Pleasure stays allowed, but becomes chosen instead of automatic.

## Product Principles

- Do not shame the user for fast dopamine.
- Do not measure, score, or diagnose the user's state.
- Do not turn focus into a punishment timer.
- Give control instead of building a cage.
- Treat repetition of the loop as more important than perfect completion.
- Use blocking only as support during focus mode.

## Tone

Dopamine120 should feel like a calm training partner: it sets up the next repetition, stays out of the way during effort, and makes the reward feel earned and deliberate.
